Why: Overview

This step shares Girl Effect’s objectives for all its brands and explains how together, they create a new normal for girls.

Global objectives

The Value, Voice and Connection framework is a set, consistent expression of what we aim to achieve as an organisation in each country where we work: it is what girls need, through the lens of what we do.

Brand DNA

Stemming from an understanding of girls' lives and the culture in which girls live we develop the insight, purpose, values and positioning of the brand. This ‘DNA’ sets the way in which we will achieve the brand objectives and forms the lens through which all content and products are designed.

Why: Overview

With a defined and consistent set of objectives, Girl Effect carries out in-country research (primary and secondary) to learn about the market landscape to enable the team to form early hypotheses on:

  • How we best stand-up a brand in market i.e. local capacity, partnership models, governance and legal etc
  • The major cultural barriers holding 13 – 15 year old girls back?
  • The culture and competitor landscape – what are other brands / media properties / NGOs doing in market? What is the opportunity for Girl Effect?
  • The cultural positioning of the brand i.e. the big hook that will resonate with our target audience
  • The best format for the brand – for example, a radio drama about a girl band that performs as a band in real-life
  • The media channels the brand should live on i.e. radio / TV / mobile etc

“Having gained insights from your research, these "tensions" in your market's culture will help you form a strategy. These tensions are potential problems that the brand can solve.”

Why: Overview

Be sure to engage HR, Finance and Ops from the start on all of the issues below for guidance on best practice

Local knowledge and expertise:

Do you understand the governance rules of your market and know them inside and out? For example, establishing our board members in Malawi?

Have you found a local fixer who knows the market, the people we need to know and the regulations?

Have you found out about local regulations on how to hire people?

Resourcing:

Have you aligned with the ops team on contracting – e.g. partners financial terms and Deliverables?

Have you allowed enough time, starting everything at least 3 months before you want to engage your first staff member?

Have you engaged with HR and Ops on staffing – visas, employment, onboarding, safety etc.?

Have you undertaken background checks on all employees including talent?

Have you undertaken formal due diligence when it comes to all partnerships or contracts you have engaged in?

Have you planned Outreach events that spread the message about what Girl Effect does to assimilate potential partners to how we do things, who we are and their expectations of us?

Girl Safeguarding:

Are you referencing our Safeguarding Policy whenever you work with girls or vulnerable parties?

Documenting:

Have you formally captured all your partner landscaping and mapping and the official framework finding partners and pitching for work?

Why: Overview

Now that you have developed your brand’s strategy and creative concept, it is important to make a prototype e.g. an episode of a drama, an edition of a magazine etc and test it in a ‘real life’ setting with the target audience to check the creative elements and media format work as you intend.

Insight from this research will enable you to modify your strategy and concept before committing to mass scale production.
